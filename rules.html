HSCLC comprises of weekly challenge rounds and an invitational timed contest. The challenge rounds will run between the October 27th and November 10th. Full solutions will be posted on the Resources page; individual results will be available on the Dashboard.

Honor Code:
Participants will not attempt to gain knowledge on aspects of a language not explicitly given in the problem statement. Problems are meant to be self-contained and are designed to assume no prior knowledge of the languages tested; to use the internet or other resources to assist in solving HSCLC problems is strictly prohibited.

Of course, we have few means of ensuring that the honor code is upheld. That is why we place full trust on participants to act in accordance to the spirit of honor code.

In Laymans Terms:
To be honest, there is no incentive to cheat. There are no monetary prizes, no official honors. Qualifying for the invitational round is fantastic, but cheating on the weekly challenges will simply render one incapable of solving the challenging questions found on the timed test.

Computational linguistics problems are meant to be engaging and enjoyable! The entire appeal in these questions is being thrown into a wholly foreign linguistic environment and using only logic and perseverance to make sense of it. Cheating on these questions only makes them less enjoyable and less worthwhile.

If you must know the answers to the challenges, feel free to use resources to obtain them; DO NOT submit the solutions as your own work.
